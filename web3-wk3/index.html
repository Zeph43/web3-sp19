User vs Design. Fight!

UX vs UI vs IA vs IxD.
4 Confusing Design Terms Defined.

Once upon a time, the word “design” overwhelmingly meant graphic design. But no longer. With new fields and job titles appearing on our ever-changing digital landscape, here’s a quick overview of 4 forms of design.

[Keep in mind that the boundaries between each of these design roles are very fluid (e.g. IxD is quite similar to UX in its approach) so these roles often overlap.]

UX UI IA IxD

User Experience Design: concened with how a user feels about the product; it's all about research and testing; enhances user satisfaction by improving the usability, accessibility, and pleasure of user & product interactions; encompasses human–computer interaction design and extends it to address all aspects of a product/service as perceived by users.

Deliverables: Wireframes, Prototypes, Storyboards, Sitemaps, Written specifications.

User Interface Design: What, where and how elements work on screen-based products; selecting the right interface elements and arranging them on the screen in a way that's readily understood and easily used; where UX Design is a more analytical and technical field, UI Design is closer to what we refer to as graphic design (sort of, anyway).

Deliverables: text fields, radio buttons, dropdown lists, buttons, toggles, date field, breadcrumbs, sliders, search fields, icons, notifications, etc.

Information architecture: Separating website/app content and dividing it into categories; structuring and organizing content in order for users to better understand where they are on a site/app.

Deliverables: Site maps, Hierarchies, Categorizations, Navigation, Metadata.

Interaction Design: Concerned with how the user and product/service act and react to one-another; defines the structure and behavior of interactive systems. Interaction Designers strive to create meaningful relationships between people and the products and services they use, from computers to mobile devices to appliances and beyond. IxD designers anticipate what users need (from first-timers to sophisticated experts), advocate for them and ensure that the final product surprises and delights them.

Deliverables: User flows, Wireframes, User interface mockups and prototypes.

Traits Of a Budding Designer.
5 Things to Emphasize.

At the 2014 IA Summit, Fred Beecher suggested that hiring managers focus more on traits than skills when interviewing entry-level UX designers.

If you're new to UX design, highlight these traits during the interview process.

  • Passion for the craft
  • Curiosity about people and technology
  • Ability to learn quickly
  • Detail-oriented nature
  • Receptiveness to feedback (willingness to accept constructive criticism and act on it)
