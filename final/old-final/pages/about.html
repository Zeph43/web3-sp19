About.

Merging fashion with the aesthetics of privacy.
This is your amor against surveillance.

Conceal

Headwear that obscures your eyes, nose-bridge, and head shape.

Reflect

Metalliferous and microscopic glass fabrics reflect signals and flash photos.

Camouflage

Makeup that contrasts with your skin tone in unusual tones and directions.

Regaining control over your privacy has never looked this good.

Clothing protects us against the threats of the biosphere, like cold or extreme heat. But now an enormous invisible network has been created on top of our existing biosphere: the infosphere.

Consisting of networks and radiowaves, this infosphere is an ever-expanding environment in which data can easily be tracked and misused by people, companies and governments. It has made it nearly impossible to control our own private information

Chameleon changes that.

Like the clothing of the past, we protect you from the bisophere; but we also offer protection from the infosphere as well. Because you should be able to choose when to be detectable.

Designers.

These aren't your parents' designers.
These are designers for the future generation.

  • Walter Van Beirendonck

    Since his early collections, Walter has been inspired by art, music and literature, all mixed with ethnic and nature influences. Considered one of the main trend-setters in fashion, his designs are very recognizable by their strong graphics, innovating cuts and unexpected color combinations.

  • Thom Browne

    Browne grew up in Allentown, Pennsylvania, where he graduated from William Allen High School. He loved fashion and sports from a young age. Browne then attended the University of Notre Dame, living in Alumni Hall. He received a degree in economics and was a member of the swim team at the university.

  • Gareth Pugh

    Pugh's trademark is his experimentation with form and volume.He often uses nonsensically shaped, wearable sculptures to "distort the human body almost beyond recognition. Pugh describes his designs as being about the struggle between lightness and darkness.
